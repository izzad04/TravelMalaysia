Sipadan Island


Known as one of the world’s top diving spots, Sipadan Island boasts pristine waters, colorful coral reefs, and abundant marine life, including turtles, sharks, and schools of fish. It is a must-visit for diving and snorkeling enthusiasts.

  • Renowned for its pristine waters and vibrant coral reefs.
  • Home to abundant marine life, including turtles, sharks, and schools of fish.
  • A top destination for diving and snorkeling enthusiasts from around the world.
  • Offers breathtaking underwater experiences in one of the world’s premier diving locations.

Kinabatangan River


This wildlife haven is ideal for river safaris, where visitors can spot rare animals like pygmy elephants, proboscis monkeys, and orangutans, as well as vibrant bird species. The Kinabatangan River offers an authentic eco-tourism experience.

  • Perfect destination for river safaris and wildlife enthusiasts.
  • Home to rare animals like pygmy elephants, proboscis monkeys, and orangutans.
  • Spot a variety of vibrant bird species in their natural habitat.
  • Offers an authentic eco-tourism experience along the scenic Kinabatangan River.

Sandakan Division, in eastern Sabah, Malaysia.


Every day

Morning Safari: Around 6:00 AM to 9:00 AM
Evening Safari: Around 4:00 PM to 7:00 PM

Mari Mari Cultural Village


Located near Kota Kinabalu, this cultural village provides an immersive look into the traditions of Sabah’s indigenous tribes. Visitors can tour traditional houses, watch cultural performances, and even participate in activities like fire-making and bamboo cooking.

  • Features tours of traditional houses showcasing unique architectural styles.
  • Visitors can enjoy vibrant cultural performances, including traditional dances and music.
  • Hands-on activities such as fire-making and bamboo cooking are available for participants.

Jalan Kionsom, Inanam, 88450 Kota Kinabalu, Sabah


Every day

Morning Session: 10:00 AM
Afternoon Session: 2:00 PM
Evening Session: 6:00 PM (includes dinner).
Advanced booking is recommended to secure a spot.

Tunku Abdul Rahman Marine Park


A cluster of five islands (Gaya, Manukan, Sapi, Mamutik, and Sulug) off the coast of Kota Kinabalu, this marine park is perfect for island hopping, snorkeling, and relaxing on sandy beaches surrounded by clear blue waters.

  • Comprises five stunning islands: Gaya, Manukan, Sapi, Mamutik, and Sulug.
  • Offers excellent opportunities for island hopping and exploring diverse marine ecosystems.
  • Popular activities include snorkeling, diving, and swimming in crystal-clear waters.
  • Features sandy beaches ideal for relaxation and picturesque views of the sea.
  • Conveniently located near Kota Kinabalu, making it an accessible getaway.
