Hinava


Hinava is a traditional Kadazan-Dusun dish, a fresh fish salad made with marinated raw fish (usually mackerel), mixed with lime juice, shallots, ginger, bird’s eye chili, and a dash of salt. It’s a popular dish served during festive occasions and gatherings.

  • Hinava is a traditional Kadazan-Dusun dish.
  • The dish consists of marinated raw fish, usually mackerel, mixed with lime juice, shallots, ginger, bird’s eye chili, and salt.
  • It’s commonly served during festive occasions and gatherings.
  • Taste: Tangy, fresh, and slightly spicy.

Tuhau


Tuhau is a fermented wild ginger, commonly used in Kadazan-Dusun cuisine. It is often served as a side dish with rice, or pickled and mixed with chili, vinegar, and other spices to add flavor to meals.

  • Tuhau is a fermented wild ginger, commonly used in Kadazan-Dusun cuisine.
  • It is typically served as a side dish with rice or pickled and mixed with chili, vinegar, and other spices to enhance the flavor of meals.
  • Taste: Spicy, sour, and slightly pungent, with an acquired taste.

Nasi Labuk


Nasi Labuk is a traditional dish consisting of rice served with a mix of dried fish, salted fish, or pickled vegetables. It is a common food in the coastal areas of Sabah.

  • Nasi Labuk is a traditional dish consisting of rice served with a mix of dried fish, salted fish, or pickled vegetables.
  • It is commonly found in the coastal areas of Sabah.
  • Taste: Savory with a salty and earthy flavor.

Bubur Sum-Sum


Bubur Sum-Sum is a sweet, creamy dessert made from rice flour, coconut milk, and sugar, often flavored with pandan leaves. It’s typically served as a snack or dessert.

  • Bubur Sum-Sum is a sweet, creamy dessert made from rice flour, coconut milk, and sugar.
  • It is often flavored with pandan leaves.
  • Taste: Sweet and smooth with a subtle coconut flavor.
