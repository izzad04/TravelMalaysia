Nasi Kandar


Nasi Kandar Nasi Kandar is a beloved Malaysian dish originating from Penang, known for its rich flavors and diverse array of side dishes. The dish consists of steamed rice served with a variety of curries, meats, and vegetables, allowing diners to create their own personalized combination.

  • Nasi Kandar originates from Penang, Malaysia, and is known for its rich and flavorful combinations.
  • The dish consists of steamed rice served with a variety of curries, meats, and vegetables.
  • Popular options include fried chicken, beef rendang, fish curry, and vegetables such as cabbage and okra.
  • Nasi Kandar is traditionally served with a selection of spicy, tangy, and aromatic sauces that enhance its flavor.
  • This dish is a must-try for anyone visiting Penang, offering a deep dive into the island's culinary traditions.

Available at restaurants across Penang, including famous spots like Line Clear, Hameediyah, and Nasi Kandar Beratur.


Varies by restaurant, typically from 10:00 AM - 12:00 AM

Laksa Penang


Laksa Penang Laksa Penang, also known as Asam Laksa, is a quintessential dish in Penang's culinary scene. This flavorful, tangy noodle soup is made with mackerel fish cooked in a rich tamarind-based broth, giving it a unique sourness.

  • Laksa Penang, also known as Asam Laksa, is a famous dish in Penang's culinary tradition.
  • The soup is made with mackerel fish and a tamarind-based broth, giving it a unique sour flavor.
  • Served with rice noodles, the dish is garnished with fresh cucumber, onions, mint, and sambal (spicy shrimp paste).
  • The combination of sweet, sour, and spicy flavors makes it a refreshing and satisfying dish.
  • Laksa Penang is often enjoyed as breakfast or lunch and can be found at various street stalls and eateries across the island.

Found throughout Penang, with popular spots including the famous Penang Road and Lorong Selamat.


Typically from 10:00 AM - 3:00 PM, depending on the stall or restaurant.

Hokkien Mee


Hokkien Mee Hokkien Mee, also known as Prawn Mee in Penang, is a flavorful noodle dish that is a local favorite. The dish features a rich, aromatic broth made from prawns, pork, and sometimes chicken, simmered for hours to extract deep flavors.

  • Hokkien Mee, also known as Prawn Mee in Penang, is a popular noodle dish.
  • The noodles are topped with prawns, sliced pork, boiled eggs, bean sprouts, and fried shallots.
  • The broth is spicy due to the addition of chili paste, making it a perfect comfort food.
  • It’s often served with sambal on the side for an extra kick of heat.

11, Malaysia, Jalan Mawar 3, 21080 Kuala Terengganu, Terengganu, Malaysia


Generally from 8:00 AM - 3:00 PM, though times may vary by stall.

Pasembor Rojak


Pasembor Rojak Pasembur is a popular Malaysian salad dish, commonly enjoyed as a light snack or a street food delicacy in Penang. This savory dish consists of a mix of crunchy, fresh ingredients such as shredded cucumber, potatoes, bean sprouts, cucumber, and deep-fried fritters like prawn crackers or tofu.

  • Pasembor Rojak is a beloved Malaysian salad dish, often enjoyed as a street food delight in Penang.
  • The dish is topped with a sweet and spicy peanut sauce that enhances its flavors.
  • Additional toppings like hard-boiled eggs, crab sticks, and prawns are often included.
  • Pasembur offers a harmonious blend of fresh, crispy, and savory textures.

Found at various street food stalls across Penang, with popular spots like Gurney Drive and the Pasembur stall near the Esplanade.


Typically available from 10:00 AM - 10:00 PM, depending on the vendor.
