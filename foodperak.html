Rendang Tok


Rendang Tok is a traditional Malay dish originating from the state of Perak, Malaysia. It is a rich and flavorful dry rendang, characterized by its thick, caramelized sauce and tender meat, usually beef. Unlike regular rendang, Rendang Tok is cooked for a longer duration, allowing the spices to deeply infuse into the meat and the sauce to reduce into a concentrated, aromatic coating.

  • Prepared with fresh coconut milk, toasted coconut (kerisik), lemongrass, galangal, and a blend of spices like cinnamon, cloves, and star anise.
  • Sweetened with gula Melaka (palm sugar) and balanced with heat from dried chilies.
  • Traditionally made for special occasions such as weddings, festivals, and celebrations.
  • Symbolizes Perak’s rich culinary heritage with its bold flavors and melt-in-your-mouth texture.
  • Typically served with steamed rice, nasi lemak, or ketupat, offering a hearty and satisfying meal.

White Coffee


White Coffee from Perak, particularly from the town of Ipoh, is a famous and beloved beverage that has become synonymous with the region’s coffee culture. Unlike traditional coffee, Ipoh White Coffee is made from coffee beans that are roasted with margarine, giving it a unique, smooth, and slightly less bitter taste compared to dark-roasted coffee. The process of roasting with margarine gives the coffee its distinct pale color, hence the name "White Coffee."

  • Made from coffee beans roasted with margarine, which gives it a smooth and slightly less bitter taste.
  • Served with sweetened condensed milk for a rich, creamy, and slightly sweet flavor profile.
  • Commonly enjoyed as a morning or afternoon drink, paired with local snacks like Kaya Toast or Ipoh's famous dim sum.
  • Available in various forms, from freshly brewed cups in local coffee shops to convenient instant versions.
  • A must-try for anyone visiting Perak, due to its smooth, mellow flavor and rich history.

Laksa Kuala


Laksa Kuala is a distinctive version of laksa, a beloved noodle soup in Malaysia, that is especially popular in Kuala Kangsar, Perak. It is a unique blend of sour, spicy, and savory flavors, typically featuring thick rice noodles served in a rich, tangy broth made from tamarind, chili, and other aromatic spices.

  • The broth is often made with fish such as mackerel, which is shredded and mixed into the soup, providing a fresh and robust flavor.
  • A signature element of Laksa Kuala is the use of tamarind, which adds a tart and zesty element to the soup, distinguishing it from other laksa variations across Malaysia.
  • Typically garnished with fresh herbs, cucumber, boiled eggs, and chili paste, offering a delightful combination of textures and flavors.
  • Commonly enjoyed as a hearty breakfast or lunch, perfect for those seeking a flavorful meal while exploring Perak.
  • Laksa Kuala offers a distinct taste of Perak’s culinary heritage, making it a must-try dish for visitors in the region.

Nasi Lemuni


Nasi Lemuni is a traditional Malay dish that is particularly popular in the state of Perak. It is a variation of the more well-known nasi lemak, but with a unique twist. The main difference lies in the use of lemuni leaves (also known as melaleuca leaves), which are added to the rice during the cooking process. The leaves impart a subtle, earthy flavor and a greenish hue to the rice, making it distinct from the usual white rice found in traditional nasi lemak.

  • The dish is served with typical nasi lemak accompaniments such as sambal (spicy chili paste), hard-boiled eggs, crispy fried anchovies, and peanuts.
  • Some versions also include additional side dishes like fried chicken, beef rendang, or grilled fish for added flavor and richness.
  • Traditionally enjoyed as a hearty breakfast or lunch, offering a nutritious and flavorful start to the day.
  • The infusion of lemuni leaves makes it a distinct dish, representing the culinary heritage of Perak.
